Prelude — The Rambunctious River

The water pump bolted to the outside of Buddy Bootz’s treehouse was doing its best to come apart in public.

The little metal box rattled against the oak boards. Pipes knocked together. A loose valve buzzed.

Something inside snapped—and a spring shot into the leaves overhead.

Buddy Bootz stood at the top of a ladder, steadying the machine with one hand and forcing a screwdriver into a seam that had already made up its mind.

“Alright,” Buddy said. “You’ve made your point.”

The pump coughed.

A blast of rusty water hit her straight in the face.

Buddy went still.

“…That felt personal.”

Below her, something crashed through the brush.

Sir Drinks-A’Lot stepped into view, looking like the morning had already disappointed him.

“I was fine,” he said, brushing off a branch, “until the forest got involved.”

It should have stopped there.

It didn’t.

there’s a river.

It shifts.
It watches.
It remembers.

And once it pulls you in—

it doesn’t let go.
